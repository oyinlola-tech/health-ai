info Understanding LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein)

LDL is often called "bad cholesterol." An LDL level of 145 mg/dL is considered borderline high (optimal is generally below 100 mg/dL). Elevated LDL can increase the risk of plaque buildup in arteries.

Regarding statins, they are standard treatment to lower LDL. Common concerns about side effects include muscle aches, but severe side effects are rare. Your doctor likely recommended this based on your overall cardiovascular risk profile, not just this single number.
