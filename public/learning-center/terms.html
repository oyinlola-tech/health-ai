LDL

Low-Density Lipoprotein

auto_awesome

Simple Explanation

Think of LDL as a fleet of delivery trucks dropping off cholesterol to the cells in your body that need it. However, if there are too many trucks on the road (high LDL), they can cause a traffic jam and leave excess cargo (cholesterol) stuck on the walls of your blood vessels. Over time, this buildup can narrow or block the vessels.

Why it matters

LDL is often called the "bad" cholesterol because high levels increase your risk for heart disease and stroke. Monitoring this number helps your doctor understand your cardiovascular risk and decide if lifestyle changes or medications are needed to keep your arteries clear.

Common Ranges

Optimal < 100 mg/dL
Near Optimal 100 - 129 mg/dL
High 160 - 189 mg/dL
